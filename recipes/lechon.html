Lechon

Description

Lechon, derived from a Spanish word for roasted suckling pig is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines. The slowly-roasted suckling pig is usually stuffed with lemongrass, tamarind, garlic, onions, and chives, and is then roasted on a large bamboo spit over an open fire.

It is traditionally served whole on a platter, at celebrations and festive events such as weddings and Christmas. Once the meat is properly roasted and falls off the bone, people tend to eat every part of the pig, and the crispy, reddish-brown, crackling skin is especially beloved. Some famous chefs even named lechon the best pig in the world, so it is definitely worth a try.

Ingredients

  • Main
    • 1 whole native pig live weight 18 to 20 kilos
    • salt and black pepper to taste
    • soy sauce
  • Glaze
    • 1 liter of Pepsi
    • If you want to achieve a red color lechon skin, 3 part of evaporated milk + 1 part of water
  • Stuffing
    • 10 bundles lemon grass tanglad
    • 1/4 cup star anise
    • 6 pieces of laurel or bay leaves cut into small parts
    • 5 cups of crushed garlic
    • 2 kilos green onion leaves
    • 8 pieces of halved saba bananas, half-cooked through boiling optional
  • Materials
    • Medium Bamboo
    • Needles and thread for stitching
    • Clean sponge

Instructions

  1. Lechon Preparation, Cleaning, Remove hair follicles from the pigs skin and the innards. Rinse the pig and clean the inside, wash and scrub the lumps of blood inside the stomach until thoroughly clean. Pour hot water inside the pig and partly slice the rib part.
  2. Pat it dry and rub the pig with salt and pepper from inside and out. Brush the inside belly with enough or little soy sauce.
  3. Once done, stuff the belly with saba bananas (plantain), anise, green onion leaves, crushed garlic and laurel leaves. Stack it with a bundle of lemon grass in the stomach center. Lock it by stitching the belly. Stitch it well so that ingredients will not spill out when you start to roast it.
  4. Prepare a mid size bamboo and skewer the pig and split roast over hot charcoal. Do not put the charcoal directly underneath the belly of the pig but over both sides, slowly churning the pig roast.
  5. Constantly glaze pig while it slowly roast with milk or pepsi using a sponge. This will make the skin extra crispy.
  6. Roast for a 3 to 4 hours until the meat is tender. Do not overcooked.
  7. The spices and the lemongrass is what really make Cebu lechon so flavourful, aromatic with rich succulent taste.
